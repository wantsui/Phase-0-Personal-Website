Commentary:

Another site that goes into great detail about the snowflake is Wikipedia's Snowflake article .

Reasons why the Wikipedia site is superior compared with this site:

  1. The Wikipedia site uses many of the same elements and tags that we learned in this past week's challenges, such as links, div, images, headings, and paragraphs, but their site is designed a lot better than this site. Wikipedia also incorporates other languages such as CSS to assist. This greatly enhances the overall look of their site.
  2. The Wikipedia article lists information in sections and helps the user navigate through different sections via links. Meanwhile, this site lists all the information by automatically aligning them to the left of the page via paragraphs, so the information can seem cluttered.
  3. The Wikipedia article on snowflakes contains a lot more information on snowflakes than I know.
